Pinuneg

The traditional Cordilleran blood sausage.

Cultural Background

Pinuneg is a native blood sausage of the Kankanaey people in the Cordillera. It is typically made during rituals or feasts when a pig is butchered. Unlike western sausages, it uses the pig's large intestine as the casing and is filled with a mixture of blood and minced meat.

Ingredients

  • Pig's blood (fresh)
  • Pig's large intestine (cleaned thoroughly)
  • Minced pork fat and meat
  • Salt
  • Onions and garlic (optional/modern addition)

Cooking Process

  1. Clean the large intestines thoroughly (this is the most crucial step).
  2. Mix the fresh blood with the minced meat, fat, and salt.
  3. Stuff the mixture into the intestines and tie the ends securely.
  4. Boil the sausages in water (often with the other meat for the feast) until cooked through.
  5. Slice and serve. It can also be dried or smoked for preservation.

Personal Insights

Pinuneg has a unique texture that is chewy yet soft. It's not as iron-heavy as one might expect from a blood dish, thanks to the fat and meat mixed in. It's a true delicacy that represents the "nose-to-tail" eating philosophy of the Cordillera.
