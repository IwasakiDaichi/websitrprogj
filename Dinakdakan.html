Dinakdakan

The original Ilocano grilled pork delicacy.

Cultural Background

Dinakdakan is an Ilocano dish that is popular in the Cordillera region. It is often compared to Sisig, but the key difference is that the meat is boiled and grilled, not fried, and the creamy texture traditionally comes from pig brain (though mayonnaise is a common modern substitute).

Ingredients

  • Pig face (maskara) and ears
  • Pig brain (or mayonnaise)
  • Ginger, minced
  • Red onions, sliced
  • Green chilies (siling haba)
  • Calamansi juice
  • Salt and pepper

Cooking Process

  1. Boil the pig face and ears until tender.
  2. Grill the boiled meat until slightly charred and crispy.
  3. Slice the meat into bite-sized pieces.
  4. In a bowl, mix the meat with ginger, onions, and chilies.
  5. Add the pig brain (boiled) or mayonnaise and toss until creamy.
  6. Season with calamansi, salt, and pepper.

Personal Insights

Dinakdakan is the ultimate comfort food for me. The smokiness from the grill combined with the creamy dressing and the kick of chili makes it irresistible. It's best enjoyed with a cold drink and good company.
