Etag

The "Igorot Ham" - preserved with salt and smoke.

Cultural Background

Etag is a traditional way of preserving pork in the Cordillera. Before refrigerators, this was how meat was kept for long periods. It involves salting the meat and then smoking it or sun-drying it. It is an essential ingredient in many Cordilleran dishes, adding a distinct savory and smoky depth.

Ingredients

  • Pork belly or shoulder (large cuts)
  • Lots of salt
  • Smoke (from specific types of wood for flavor)

Preparation Process

  1. Rub the pork generously with salt.
  2. Cure the meat in a closed container for several days (3-7 days) to draw out moisture.
  3. Hang the meat to dry in the sun or smoke it over a fire for several weeks.
  4. Store in a cool, dry place. It can last for months.
  5. To cook, wash off the excess salt and boil it with other ingredients.

Personal Insights

Etag is an acquired taste for some due to its strong smell and salty flavor, but for us, it is the taste of home. It transforms a simple vegetable stew into a feast. The best Etag is aged properly, where the fat turns yellowish and the meat becomes dark red.
