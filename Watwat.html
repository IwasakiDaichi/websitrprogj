Watwat

The meat of celebration and community sharing.

Cultural Background

Watwat is simply boiled pork, but its significance goes beyond the recipe. It is the meat served during a "Canao" or "Kanyaw" (feast/festival). Large cauldrons of pork are boiled, and the meat is distributed to the community. It represents sharing, abundance, and unity.

Ingredients

  • Pork (large cuts, often with fat and skin)
  • Water
  • Salt (optional, as it is sometimes cooked without salt for ritual purposes)

Cooking Process

  1. Clean the pork thoroughly.
  2. Boil water in a large cauldron (kawa).
  3. Add the pork and boil until tender.
  4. No spices or vegetables are typically added, to preserve the pure taste of the meat and to ensure it doesn't spoil quickly during the distribution.

Personal Insights

Receiving a piece of Watwat is a sign that you are part of the community. It's not about the culinary complexity; it's about the spirit of the gathering. The meat is often tough but flavorful, carrying the essence of the celebration.
