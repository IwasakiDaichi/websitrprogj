Benguet Coffee: Brewed from the Highlands

Benguet Coffee is grown in the rich soils of the Cordillera mountains, giving it a robust, earthy flavor and aroma that coffee lovers adore. Whether for a morning boost or a relaxing afternoon, a cup of Benguet Coffee brings the mountains right to your cup.


Cultural Background

Benguet Coffee is grown in the highlands of the Cordillera, nurtured by local farmers using traditional methods passed down for generations. It’s not just a crop—it represents livelihood, pride, and a deep connection to the land. The coffee’s robust aroma and earthy flavor make it a regional treasure.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons Benguet coffee beans (medium roast)
  • 1 cup water
  • Optional: sugar or milk

Cooking Process

  1. Grind the beans: Grind Benguet coffee beans to a medium-coarse texture.
  2. Boil water: Heat water to just below boiling (around 90–95°C or 194–203°F).
  3. Brew: Place coffee grounds in a French press or drip coffee maker. Pour hot water over the grounds and let steep for 4 minutes.
  4. Serve: Press the plunger (if using a French press) and pour into a cup. Add sugar or milk if desired.
  5. Extract the wine: After fermentation, strain the liquid from the rice. This liquid is your tapuy. Chill and serve.

Personal Insights

For me, Benguet Coffee is more than a morning beverage. Drinking it while watching the sunrise over the mountains connects me to the patience and dedication of the farmers who grew it. Pairing it with patupat or a small serving of dinakdakan creates a full Cordillera experience that feels deeply grounding.
