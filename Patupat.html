Patupat

A Sweet Cordilleran Rice Delicacy.

Cultural Background

Patupat is a sweet sticky rice treat wrapped in woven coconut leaves and boiled in sugarcane juice, giving it a rich flavor and unique aroma. It is often served during special occasions and festivals in the Cordillera and Ilocos regions.

Ingredients

  • Glutinous rice (malagkit)
  • Woven coconut leaf pouches
  • Sugarcane juice (or brown sugar + water)
  • Pinch of salt

Cooking Process

  1. Wash the glutinous rice thoroughly.
  2. Fill each coconut leaf pouch halfway with rice (to allow expansion).
  3. Prepare a large pot with sugarcane juice (or sugar water mixture).
  4. Place the pouches inside and boil for several hours.
  5. Cook until the rice becomes sticky and fully absorbs the sweetness.
  6. Remove from the pot and let it cool before serving.

Personal Insights

The secret to a great Patupat is the quality of the sugarcane juice and the patience in boiling it. Longer boiling results in a richer, caramel-like flavor. It's best eaten as a snack or dessert, and the weaving of the pouches is an art form in itself.
