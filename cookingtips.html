Cooking tips

Here are some cooking tips, hope it can help.

Read the recipe

Before you begin, read the entire recipe to understand the steps and have all the necessary ingredients and equipment ready.


Practice mise en place

French for "everything in its place," this means prepping and measuring all ingredients before you start cooking to ensure a smoother process.


Use a scale for dry ingredients

A kitchen scale provides more consistent and accurate results than volume cups, especially for flour.


Organize your workspace

Keep your workspace clean and tidy by having a waste bowl nearby for scraps. This is especially useful for saving trips to the bin.


Secure your cutting board

To prevent your cutting board from slipping, place a damp paper towel or tea towel underneath it.


Don’t Be Afraid to Try New Things

While you should start out by cooking dishes you like, don’t be afraid to branch out as well. Doing so can help you discover new favorite recipes. You know what you and your family like.
